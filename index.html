more about me

I was born in 1993 in the small town Dzerzhinsk, Nizhny Novgorod region. Even at school, it turned out that I prefer subjects where you need a technical mindset. This played an important role in the choice of the university. All my relatives on my father's side were railway workers. I decided to continue the dynasty. In 2010, I entered the St. Petersburg State University of Railways named after Emperor Alexander I, the Faculty of "Automation and Intelligent Technologies", the Department of "Automation and Telemechanics and Communication in Railway Transport". In 2015, he defended his diploma with honors on the topic "Technological development of a simulation model for automatic crossing alarm systems". After studying, I started my career in Nizhny Novgorod. I got my first position as an electrical engineer in August 2015. My responsibilities included: maintenance, repairs, installation of new electrical appliances, and maintenance of documentation. A year later, I received a position as a technologist of the 2nd category and was transferred to the center for diagnostics and monitoring in the Gorky branch of the central Directorate of the railway. My duties included: diagnostics and control of electrical appliances, as well as remote assistance in troubleshooting technical problems. I also had hobbies, among which I spent most of my time learning German, as many of my friends live in Germany and I just like this country and language. I must say that despite the rapid development of a career, I also had a dream - to become a programmer. In July 2018, I decided to finish my career at Russian Railways, where in general, my work experience was 3 years. At the end of the same year, I decided to start realizing my long-held dream and got a national visa to Germany to improve my language skills. I spent the next year in Duisburg, Nordrhein-Westfallen studying German, in 2019 I received a certificate of proficiency at the "C1" level from the Language School "Communikation Akoun & Scholten" GmbH, returned to Russia and received a "TestDaF" certificate, with which I could enter any university in German-speaking countries. In 2020, with the beginning of the pandemic and the closing of borders, I decided not to waste time, but to use it to study a new specialty "Frontend Developer" at the online university "SkillBox". Thanks to a well-chosen training structure, I managed to master a huge part of this specialty in less than a year, and I really like to write code in JavaScript, make up and understand new aspects of programming. In conclusion, I can say that at the moment, most of my time I continue to develop in the Frontend direction, constantly trying to apply new knowledge, learn English and try to maintain German. Of course, this is not all that I could tell about myself, in the end, I am always ready to answer your clarifying questions.
